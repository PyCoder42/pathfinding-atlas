How does Google Maps actually work?

An interactive lab for the algorithms behind modern routing — from textbook Dijkstra all the way to Customizable Contraction Hierarchies. Watch them race on a fake Google-Maps road network and on enormous weighted graphs, then read the guides that explain exactly what you're seeing.

🗺️ Explore the Map 🕸️ Attack the Graphs 📖 Read the Guides
🗺️

Real-world mapping

A procedurally generated road network with named cities, highways, arterials and local streets — weighted by travel time, so the shortest path is the fastest route. Pick two cities, add rush-hour traffic, and race the algorithms a real router would use.
🕸️

Weighted graphs & mazes

Mazes, weighted terrain grids, huge geometric meshes, and even a negative-weight graph for Bellman–Ford. Scale them into the hundreds of thousands of nodes and feel why preprocessing wins.
